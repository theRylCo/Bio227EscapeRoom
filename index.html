The Duskwalker is not “saved forever.” That would be too neat. But the population has habitat, gene flow, safer release plans, local support, and a future strategy that can bend when the climate does.

This is the main point of the game: conservation works best as a connected system, not a single heroic fix.

Point of the Game :)
I built an escape-room game where each puzzle represents a conservation decision. The rooms move from ex situ rescue, to protected-area design, to conservation in working landscapes, to restoration ecology, and finally to future policy/funding decisions. The player has to combine biological accuracy with practical tradeoffs, which mirrors the way conservation biology depends on genetics, behavior, habitat, economics, enforcement, and public support.
